It's a triangle of opportunity

A perfectly normal, industry-standard, totally-not-illegal-in-most-jurisdictions structure.

One person at the top. Everyone else below them. Just like nature.

Some critics ask: "Is this a pyramid scheme?" The answer is no — pyramid schemes are illegal. Pyramide* is a Multi-Level Customer Acquisition System (MLCAS), which is a completely different shape. (A triangle.)

  • Level 1: You generate a code. No purchase necessary. Free as a bird.
  • Levels 2–7: Everyone below you funnels commissions up the structure forever.
  • Top of pyramid: A small number of Sapphire L5s who got in early. They are in Tulum.
  • Bottom of pyramid: Statistically, you. But it could be different! Probably won't be.

Frequently asked accusations

We've heard them all. We have legally-vetted answers.

Is this a pyramid scheme?

No. Pyramid schemes are illegal. Pyramide* is a Multi-Level Customer Acquisition System, which is a fundamentally different geometric structure (one is a 3D solid; the other is a "go-to-market strategy"). Our legal team confirms we are almost certainly not a pyramid scheme in 42 of 50 US states. We are actively appealing the other 8.

Wait — there's no cost to join?

Correct. Pyramide* is completely free. Anyone can generate a code in one click. The MLM mechanic doesn't need an upfront fee when the downline pays for itself through the seven-level commission structure. Our legal team is delighted by this and may sleep tonight.

Merch credit vs. cash — what's the difference?

On Starter tier, all rewards are credit at the PostHog merch store (stickers, tees, the good hoodie, the mediocre tote). At Platinum L1+, you can flip the toggle and take rewards as cash via Stripe Connect. Most people don't because the hoodie is genuinely excellent.

What counts as an "enterprise" referral?

Officially: a company signing an annual contract for 100+ seats. Unofficially: accelerator cohorts (every portfolio co counts separately — yes, that means one YC batch is thirty enterprise referrals in a trench coat), anyone with "VP", "Chief", or "Director of Procurement" in their title, and — somehow — that guy with the podcast. Verified by Pyramide*'s compliance hedgehog. No upper cap. Refer Microsoft and we will personally send you a fruit basket.

How much will I really earn?

Per our income disclosure: 87% of members earned $0 or less last year. "Or less" because of the Starter Kit fee opportunity cost. The top 0.1% earned a lot. Statistically you will not be in the top 0.1%, but somebody has to be.

Wait — is this a real product or a hackathon mockup?

It's a hackathon mockup. A team at PostHog was building a customer referral feature and somebody said "what if it was a pyramid scheme but ironic." Now here we are. Please do not actually use Pyramide*. We mean it. There is no Pyramide. There is no Brad.

Can I leave?

Click "deactivate." Your code stops working immediately. Your downline reverts to your upline. The lingering social damage of having sent your college roommates a recruitment link at 2am, however, is non-refundable. Circle of life.

Your downline isn't going to recruit itself.

(It will, actually — that's the whole pitch — but you have to start the chain.)
