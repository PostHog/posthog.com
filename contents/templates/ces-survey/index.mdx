          <h5 class="text-xl font-extrabold m-0 pb-1 pr-4">What's a good CES score?</h5>
          <p class="m-0 text-[15px]">CES measures how difficult your product is to use on a 1-5 scale. Higher is better, and 4+ is considered a strong result.</p>
        </li>
        <li class="relative md:max-w-md py-4 md:py-8 pl-4 pr-2 md:px-12">
          <h5 class="text-xl font-extrabold m-0 pb-1 pr-4">Why is CES important?</h5>
          <p class="m-0 text-[15px]"><a href="https://hbr.org/2010/07/stop-trying-to-delight-your-customers">Studies</a> have shown CES is a better indicator of customer loyalty than many other metrics and correlates to retention.</p>
        </li>
        <li class="relative md:max-w-md py-4 md:py-8 pl-4 pr-2 md:px-12">
          <h5 class="text-xl font-extrabold m-0 pb-1 pr-4">Tips for using CES surveys</h5>
          <p class="m-0 text-[15px]">CES surveys reveal ease of use, but not overall value. They should be used alongside <a href="/templates/nps-survey">NPS surveys</a> to get the full picture.</p>
        </li>
        <li class="relative md:max-w-md py-4 md:py-8 pl-4 pr-2 md:px-12">
          <h5 class="text-xl font-extrabold m-0 pb-1 pr-4">When to use CES surveys</h5>
          <p class="m-0 text-[15px]">CES surveys are especially useful for new users, and work great when deployed after onboarding flows.</p>
        </li>
      </ul>
