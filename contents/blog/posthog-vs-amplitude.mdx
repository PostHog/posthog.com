Want to know how PostHog and Amplitude compare? If you remember nothing else, remember these two things:

1. **Amplitude** is a product analytics tool built for non-technical users and enterprise data teams.

2. **PostHog** is a comprehensive, all-in-one platform built for engineers that replaces Amplitude _and_ numerous other tools.

## How is PostHog different?

### 1. We're an all-in-one platform

PostHog is more than a product analytics tool, it's an all-in-one platform that replaces multiple tools in your data stack. You could run Amplitude for analytics, [LaunchDarkly](/blog/posthog-vs-launchdarkly) for feature flags, Hotjar for session recording, [Statsig](/blog/posthog-vs-statsig) for A/B testing, or you could just use PostHog for everything. One platform, one bill: seamless integration.

### 2. We're built for engineers

We want engineers to engineer their products, not their data. That means giving them all the tools they need to make decisions autonmously. PostHog autocaptures data, so you don't need to waste energy instrumenting basic events – we also offer robust tools for defining custom events. Our pricing is transparent and we're self-serve. You can setup PostHog and start paying us without ever speaking to a sales rep, though we have an [awesome customer success team](/contact-sales) if you have questions.

### 3. We're open source and transparent

Our MIT License isn't just for show. You can read [our source code](https://github.com/PostHog/posthog), raise issues and PRs, and build your own integrations. We're also a truly open-source company. Our [company handbook](/handbook) is open to everyone, as is [how we pay people](/handbook/people/compensation), our [long-term mission and strategy](/handbook/why-does-posthog-exist), and our [product roadmap](/roadmap). Got a question about anything? Ask in [our online community](/questions).

### Pricing

PostHog charges for actual usage – i.e. product analytics events, feature flag API requests, and so on. No credit card is required to signup and use PostHog, but entering a credit card unlocks almost all features and **you'll pay nothing** if you stay within our **generous free tier** limits:

|                       | **Free usage per month** 
|-----------------------|--------------------------
| **Product analytics** | 1 million events        
| **Session replay**    | 5,000 recordings         
| **Feature flags**     | 1 million API requests
| **A/B testing**       | 1 million API requests  
| **Surveys**           | 250 responses            

Amplitude charges based on monthly tracked users (MTUs). The cost of add owns, such as A/B testing (not available on self-serve plans), group analytics, and unlimited cohorts (self-serve plans are limited to five), isn't disclosed.

You can integrate PostHog into your website using Google Tag Manager, and we're currently building an [easy to use web analytics product](/docs/web-analytics). ### How can I estimate my usage?

It's simpler than you think. 

The easiest way is to [sign up to PostHog](https://us.posthog.com/signup), integrate our snippet, then check the projection on your billing page after a few days.

Alternatively, you can guesstimate by multiplying your current monthly active users by an estimate of events generated per user – 50 to 100 per user is a good starting point. See [Estimating usage & costs](/docs/billing/estimating-usage-costs) in our docs for more.

### Does PostHog block bots by default?

Yes. See the [full blocklist in our docs](/troubleshooting#does-posthog-block-bots-by-default).

### Can I use PostHog with a CDP? (Segment, Rudderstack, etc.)

Yes. See [Using PostHog with a CDP](/docs/advanced/cdp) in our docs.

### What about ad blockers?

We recommend all users deploy a reverse proxy, which enables you send events to PostHog Cloud using your own domain. Events sent from your own domain and are less likely to be intercepted by tracking blockers, ensuring you capure the best data possible.

We have [reverse proxy setup guides](/docs/advanced/proxy) for AWS Cloudfront, Caddy, Cloudflare, Netlify, Vercel, and more, in our docs.
