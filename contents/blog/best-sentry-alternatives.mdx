[Sentry](/blog/posthog-vs-sentry) is one of the most widely-used error tracking tools on the market, and for good reason. It's mature, reliable, and most developers have used it at some point.

But Sentry isn't the only option. Whether you're looking for deeper product context, a more generous free tier, or a tool that combines error tracking with analytics and session replay, there are [strong alternatives worth considering](/blog/best-error-tracking-tools).

In this guide, we compare the best Sentry alternatives, including tools that go beyond traditional error monitoring to help you understand *who* is affected and *why* it matters â€“ not just what broke.

## 1. PostHog

- **Founded:** 2020
- **Similar to:** Sentry, LogRocket
- **Typical users:** Engineers and product teams
- **Typical customers:** Mid-size B2Bs and startups

![PostHog](https://res.cloudinary.com/dmukukwp6/image/upload/Clean_Shot_2025_02_24_at_11_05_43_2x_4bd442ec11.png)

### What is PostHog?

[PostHog](/) (that's us ðŸ‘‹) is an all-in-one platform combining [error tracking](/error-tracking), [session replays](/session-replay), [product analytics](/product-analytics), [web analytics](/web-analytics), [A/B testing](/experiments), [feature flags](/feature-flags), [user surveys](/surveys), [LLM observability](/llm-analytics), and more into one product. 

This means it's not only an alternative to [Sentry](/blog/posthog-vs-sentry) but also tools like [Amplitude](/blog/posthog-vs-amplitude) and [LaunchDarkly](/blog/posthog-vs-launchdarkly).

#### Key features

- **Error tracking:** [Capture errors and exceptions](/docs/error-tracking/installation). Provide stack traces to debug issues. Correlate with product usage data.

- **Session replays:** View exactly how users are using your site. Includes event timelines, console logs, network activity, and 90-day data retention.

- **Product analytics:** Custom trends, funnels, user paths, retention analysis, and segment user cohorts. Also, direct [SQL querying](/docs/product-analytics/sql) for power users.

- **Feature flags:** Rollout features safely with [local evaluation](/docs/feature-flags/local-evaluation) (for faster performance), JSON payloads, and instant rollbacks.

- **A/B tests:** Optimize your app and website with up to nine test variations and track impact on primary and secondary metrics. Automatically calculate test duration, sample size, and statistical significance.

- **Surveys:** Target surveys by event or user properties. Templates for [net promoter score (NPS)](/templates/nps-survey), [product-market fit (PMF)](/templates/pmf-survey) surveys, and more.

<details>
  <summary>What is Sentry used for?</summary>

**Sentry** is an error tracking and performance monitoring platform that helps developers detect, diagnose, and fix bugs in production. It captures exceptions with detailed stack traces, groups similar errors, and integrates with developer tools like GitHub and Slack for streamlined workflows.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Why look for Sentry alternatives?</summary>

Common reasons include: needing more context around errors, wanting a more generous free tier, consolidating multiple tools into one platform, or finding Sentry's pricing too expensive as you scale.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>What's the best Sentry alternative overall?</summary>

**PostHog** is the best all-around Sentry alternative for most teams. It combines [error tracking](/error-tracking) with [session replay](/session-replay), [product analytics](/product-analytics), [feature flags](/feature-flags), [A/B testing](/experiments), [LLM analytics](/llm-analytics), and [surveys](/surveys), giving you a complete view of both the technical issue and its user impact.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Which Sentry alternatives are open source?</summary>

**PostHog** is fully open source under the MIT license with a public roadmap. **Sentry** itself is also open source (with a more restrictive license) and offers self-hosting. Most other alternatives like **Rollbar**, **Raygun**, **LogRocket**, and **AppSignal** are proprietary and cloud-only.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Which Sentry alternative has the best free tier?</summary>

**PostHog** offers 100k free errors/month, plus 1M free analytics events, 5k session replays, and unlimited team members. **New Relic** offers 100 GB/month of data ingest. **Sentry's** free tier is limited to 5k errors and 1 user.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Which Sentry alternative is best for frontend debugging?</summary>

**LogRocket** excels at frontend debugging with visual session replays tied to errors. **PostHog** also offers session replay with error correlation, plus broader product analytics capabilities.

</details>

<details>
  <summary>Which Sentry alternative is best for enterprise observability?</summary>

**Datadog** and **New Relic** are built for enterprise-scale observability, covering infrastructure, APM, logs, and metrics across your entire stack. Both can get expensive at scale, but offer deep visibility into complex systems.

</details>
