## What is the PostHog MCP?

The MCP is a server your AI coding agent talks to. You ask a question in English. It runs the query against your PostHog data. The answer shows up in your editor. No SQL, no dashboards, no UI. Just a question, and an answer.

Things you can ask include:

- _"How many unique users signed up in the last 7 days, broken down by day?"_
- _"Create an A/B test for our pricing page that measures conversion to checkout."_
- _"What are the top 5 errors in my project this week?"_

## What does the MCP actually do?

There are 50+ tools spanning product analytics, error tracking, session replay, feature flags, experiments, dashboards, and HogQL. Your agent picks the right one and calls it. You stay in your editor.

## Aren't dashboards good enough?

Dashboards are useful when you know what you want to look at. Most of the time, you don't. You're writing code, you have one question, and you don't want to switch apps to answer it.

That's what the MCP fixes. The dashboards are still there when you want them.

## The future is no UI

PostHog is a big platform, with a lot of tools. We built the MCP because we kept seeing comments like those below - that the UI is overwhelming, or that people didn't understand all the things PostHog can do. 

Even our own marketing team found it [hard to keep up with how fast we ship things](/newsletter/hidden-danger-of-shipping-fast).

We could have done a big marketing campaign, or we could have got improved our UI iteratively and slowly - but we think the future is no UI at all.

Instead: Ask a question. Get an answer.
