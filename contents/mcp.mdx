## What is the PostHog MCP?

The MCP is a server your AI coding agent talks to. You ask a question in English. It runs the query against your PostHog data. The answer shows up in your editor. No SQL, no dashboards, no tickets.

<ExplainerVideo />

Free, open source, works with every editor you actually use.

## What does the MCP actually do?

There are 50+ tools spanning product analytics, error tracking, session replay, feature flags, experiments, dashboards, and HogQL. Your agent picks the right one and calls it. You stay in your editor.

<MCPFaqGrid />

Want _more_? [See the full list of tools](/docs/model-context-protocol).

## Aren't dashboards good enough?

Dashboards are useful when you know what you want to look at. Most of the time, you don't. You're writing code, you have one question, and you don't want to switch apps to answer it.

That's what the MCP fixes. The dashboards are still there when you want them.

## Why did we build this?

We kept watching engineers (and ourselves) tab over to PostHog to ask the same questions and forget the same event names. The MCP gives that job to the agent. It's faster for everyone, including the engineer who used to be the bottleneck.

The dashboards stay. We just stopped needing most of them.

More on the why in [Machine, copy, paste](/blog/machine-copy-paste-mcp-intro).
