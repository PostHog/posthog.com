Spain adds a compliance layer that most other EOR countries don't: a **collective bargaining agreement (convenio colectivo)** that can override standard employment law on probation, leave, sick pay, and working hours. Spain also has specific **remote work legislation** (Ley 10/2021) that applies to any employee working remotely ≥30% of their hours — which is every PostHog team member.

---

## Payroll blockers (start early)

- [ ] **DNI** (Spanish citizens) or **NIE** (foreigners) — the tax identification number. EU citizens can start with passport while NIE is processed, but **NIE must be obtained within 90 days**
- [ ] **Social Security number** — Deel registers the employee from day one
- [ ] **Spanish bank account** — required for salary payments
- [ ] **Mod. 145 tax form** — required for correct tax (IRPF) withholding
- [ ] **Remote work agreement** — mandatory written agreement under Ley 10/2021. Must include: equipment, expenses, working hours, digital disconnection, data protection, duration, and reversibility. **Must be reviewed annually** `[PostHog: confirm Deel handles this]`
- [ ] **H&S training** — mandatory occupational risk prevention training, required by law for all employees upon hire
- [ ] **Medical check authorization/waiver** — employer must offer voluntary medical check. Employee must accept or waive in writing

## Probation (Periodo de prueba)

- **6 months** — set by CBA (statutory maximum for qualified technical staff is 6 months) `[PostHog: confirm CBA provision via Deel]`
- During probation: either party can terminate **immediately, with no notice, no reason, and no compensation**
- **Key difference from other countries:** If the employee takes extended sick leave, parental leave, or other extended leave during probation, the **probation clock pauses** and restarts when they return
- Same rights and obligations as all other employees during probation

## Leave

### Annual leave (Vacaciones)
- Statutory minimum: **30 calendar days** (~22 working days). CBA may provide more
- PostHog's policy is **unlimited, permissionless time off** with a minimum expectation of **25 days/year** (including national holidays). This exceeds the Spanish statutory minimum. In practice, the Deel contract will specify the statutory 30 calendar days as the floor
- Spain has **14 public holidays** per year: 8 national, 2 autonomous community, 2 local, 2 additional. Specific days vary by region. Team members should add their regional holidays via PTO by Deel's "Bulk Add by Region" feature
- **No carryover.** Use-it-or-lose-it within the calendar year. No in-lieu payment for unused days (unless termination)

### Parental leave — **⚠️ Updated July 2025**
- **19 weeks per parent** (increased from 16 weeks by Royal Decree-Law 9/2025, effective 31 July 2025): 17 weeks birth leave + 2 weeks paid parental leave
- **Single parents:** 32 weeks total
- **First 6 weeks:** Mandatory, uninterrupted, full-time, immediately after birth/adoption
- Remaining weeks: flexible within first 12 months, full-time or part-time
- **2 additional weeks** (per parent) can be deferred until child turns 8
- **100% paid** by Social Security (based on contribution base)
- Notice: **15 days** for each period of leave
- Eligibility: 180 days of Social Security contributions in prior 7 years (or 360 days lifetime)
- Additional per child: +1 week per parent for each child after the first
- Spanish parental leave (19 weeks) is less generous than PostHog's global maternity policy for team members with 12+ months tenure (up to 24 weeks). For paternity, Spanish law (19 weeks) is significantly more generous than PostHog's global policy (6 weeks), so Spanish law applies. The key principle: whichever is more generous applies
- Communicate parental leave to Fraser at least **4 months** before it begins

### Child feeding permit (Permiso de lactancia)
- 1 hour per day (can split into 2 × 30 min) until child is 9 months, OR accumulate into **15 calendar days** taken immediately after parental leave
- Paid by employer (not Social Security)
- Can extend to 12 months if both parents exercise the right

### Sick leave (Incapacidad temporal)
- **Days 1-3:** Unpaid (statutory). CBA may improve this `[PostHog: confirm CBA sick pay terms via Deel]`
- **Days 4-20:** 60% of contribution base
- **Day 21+:** 75% of contribution base
- CBA may require employer to top up to 100% from day 5 for leaves >30 days `[PostHog: confirm via Deel]`
- Doctor submits sick note directly to Social Security (employee doesn't need to provide paperwork since April 2023)
- Maximum: **365 days**, extendable by 180 days by INSS medical tribunal
- **If sick during holiday:** Cancel holiday days, rebook as sick leave. Holiday entitlement preserved
- For extended illness (5+ work days), contact Fraser so People & Ops can plan

### Unpaid leave
- **Excedencia for childcare:** Up to 3 years per child. First year: same position reserved. After: equivalent position
- **Excedencia for family care:** Up to 2 years for dependent relative (2nd degree consanguinity/affinity)
- **Excedencia voluntaria:** After 1 year, voluntary leave of absence for 4 months to 5 years. Contract suspended — no salary, no Social Security. Preferential right to reinstatement only
- **Permiso sin sueldo:** If included in applicable CBA — typically after 1 year of service, up to 1 month unpaid leave per year `[PostHog: confirm if available under Deel's CBA]`

### Other paid leave
- Marriage: **15 calendar days**
- Death of spouse/parent/child: **4 days** (2 days for other 2nd-degree relatives; +2 if travel required)
- Residential move: **1 day**
- Prenatal exams/adoption preparation: necessary time

## Relocations

### Moving TO Spain
- Need: DNI or passport + NIE, Social Security number, Spanish bank account
- EU citizens can start with passport, NIE within 90 days
- New contract (via Deel), Mod. 145 tax form, H&S training completion
- Non-EU: must have non-sponsored work permit

### Moving FROM Spain
- Sign termination agreement and final paycheck
- Suggested: deregister from padrón municipal (town hall) and AEAT (tax agency), close Spanish bank accounts
- Deel handles Social Security notification and certificate of withheld taxes

## Health and safety

- External prevention service required by law (covers work medicine, safety, industrial hygiene, ergonomic/psycho-social risks) — Deel handles this via their EOR infrastructure
- **Medical check:** Employer must offer; employee can accept or waive in writing (required at onboarding)
- **H&S training:** Mandatory annual training for all employees. Required by law and records must be kept
- **Remote work:** Home workspace must meet basic safety standards. Assessments can be done remotely; in-person visits require employee consent
- PostHog provides all team members with a company Brex card for equipment to ensure an ergonomic home setup

## Notice period

- **Employee resignation:** **15 working days** (not calendar days). Shortfall can be deducted from final payslip
- **Employer termination:** Varies by reason — typically 15 days for objective dismissal. CBA may require more
- PostHog's default is **30 days** voluntary notice. For Spain, whichever is applicable under local law/CBA applies
- Accrued vacation can be taken during notice period (coordinate with team lead for handover)

---

## Time tracking

- **Mandatory** since 2019 (Royal Decree-Law 8/2019). Must record daily start and end times
- Maximum: **9 hours/day**, max **80 overtime hours/year**
- Records must be kept for 4 years
- PostHog operates on trust and doesn't count hours, but Spain's legal obligation still applies. Deel should provide the time-tracking mechanism as part of EOR compliance `[PostHog: confirm how this is handled]`

### Jornada Intensiva — August reduced hours
- If the applicable CBA includes it: employees may work a **reduced continuous schedule** during August (max 36 hours/week, distributed with minimum breaks) `[PostHog: confirm if CBA provides this via Deel]`
- Salary and full-time status unaffected
- The hours difference from regular schedule must be compensated over the year

## Leaving PostHog

- **Voluntary:** 15 working days notice (Spanish statutory). Send resignation to people@posthog.com
- **Involuntary:** PostHog offers 4 months of base salary (includes any legally required notice period) for team members with 3+ months tenure. Spanish severance for unfair dismissal is **33 days' salary per year of service** (capped at 24 months' pay); for objective dismissal: 20 days per year (capped at 12 months). Whichever is greater applies
- Final payslip must include: prorated extra pay (paga extra), unused vacation, any pending items
- Offboarding issue created from GitHub template by People & Ops

## Remote work agreement (Ley 10/2021)

This is specific to Spain and **mandatory** for any employee working remotely ≥30% of hours over a 3-month reference period — which is every PostHog team member.

The written agreement must include:
- Equipment and tools inventory
- Expense compensation mechanism (per CBA or individual agreement)
- Working hours and availability schedule
- Work location
- Duration and reversibility terms
- Digital disconnection policy
- Data protection measures
- Monitoring/control methods

**Must be reviewed annually.** `[PostHog: confirm Deel handles creation and annual review]`
