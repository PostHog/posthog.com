The Netherlands is straightforward operationally but has one time-sensitive trap: the **30% ruling** application window. Miss it by a week and the team member loses thousands of euros in tax benefits over 5 years. This page exists to make sure that doesn't happen.

---

## Payroll blockers (start early)

- [ ] **BSN number (burgerservicenummer)** — issued at municipality (gemeente) registration. Required for payroll, bank accounts, health insurance, everything. Gemeente appointment wait times can be **2-3 months** in Amsterdam/Rotterdam — book immediately
- [ ] **Dutch health insurance** — compulsory within **4 months** of registering. No public option — all private. Average ~€135/month (2025). Failure to sign up = government fine (€426 in 2023). Backdated to registration date. PostHog offers private health insurance in the Netherlands via Deel — check Deel platform for current provider
- [ ] **Dutch bank account** — required for salary. Can't open without BSN
- [ ] **30% ruling application** — must be submitted within **4 months** of start date for retroactive application. See [30% ruling section](#30-ruling) below — this is the highest-stakes item on this list
- [ ] **Registered address** — must register at gemeente within **5 days** of arrival if staying >4 months
- [ ] **DigiD** — digital identity for government services. Set up after BSN is received

## Onboarding

- Right-to-work documentation (passport, residence permit, or EU Blue Card)
- Mutually signed employment contract (via Deel)
- BSN number
- Registered Dutch address
- Dutch bank account details
- Dutch health insurance confirmation (within 4 months)

### Documents for gemeente registration
- Valid passport or ID (no driving licenses)
- Residence permit (if applicable)
- Rental contract
- Certified birth certificate (must be in Dutch, English, French, or German — otherwise official translation with apostille required)
- Marriage/partnership/divorce certificate (if applicable)

## Probation

- **2 months** for indefinite contracts (PostHog standard via Deel). This is the legal maximum
- 1 month max for fixed-term contracts >6 months but <2 years
- **No probation** allowed for contracts ≤6 months
- During probation: either party can terminate **immediately, without notice or reason**
- **Cannot be extended** — if you have concerns, raise them before the 2-month mark
- **Ops action:** Set a reminder 2 weeks before probation ends to confirm with manager

## Leave

### Annual leave
- Statutory minimum: **20 days/year** (5-day week). PostHog's policy is **unlimited, permissionless time off** with a minimum expectation of **25 days/year** (including national holidays), which exceeds the Dutch statutory minimum
- **Carryover:** Statutory leave carries over to **30 June** of the following year (6 months, not March like Germany). Expires after that
- No in-lieu payment for unused statutory days while employed — must be taken
- Book time off via PTO by Deel Slack app. Manually add Dutch public holidays you plan to take using the "Bulk Add by Region" feature

### Maternity leave
- **16 weeks** total, 100% paid (by UWV, not employer): 4-6 weeks before birth (zwangerschapsverlof) + 10-12 weeks after (bevallingsverlof)
- If baby is born after due date, total leave can exceed 16 weeks
- Dutch maternity leave (16 weeks) is less generous than PostHog's global policy for team members with 12+ months tenure (up to 24 weeks). In that case, PostHog's policy applies. For team members with <12 months, compare Dutch statutory vs PostHog policy and apply whichever is more generous

### Partner/paternity leave
- **1 week** immediately after birth (100% paid, within 4 weeks of birth)
- **5 additional weeks** within first 6 months (70% paid by UWV, capped at 70% of max daily wage)
- PostHog's global paternity leave (6 weeks at 12+ months tenure) may exceed the Dutch paid entitlement — apply whichever is more generous

### Parental leave (Ouderschapsverlof)
- Calculation: **26 × weekly hours**. So a 40hr/week employee gets 1,040 hours
- **9 weeks** must be taken within child's first year — paid at **70% of daily wage** (capped) by UWV
- Remaining weeks: unpaid, can be taken until child's **8th birthday**

### Adoption/foster leave
- **6 weeks** within 26 weeks of child's arrival (100% paid)

### Sick leave
- Employer pays **70% of salary** for up to **104 weeks** (2 years). Most employment contracts/CBAs supplement to higher %
- No doctor's note required — but company doctor (Arbo service) will contact the employee to assess and manage return-to-work `[PostHog: confirm Arbo provider via Deel]`
- If sick during holiday: employee can cancel holiday days and rebook as sick leave (holiday days preserved)
- For extended illness (5+ work days), contact Fraser so People & Ops can plan

### Care leave
- **Short-term:** 2 × weekly hours per 12 months, 70% paid. Must be only available caregiver
- **Long-term:** 6 × weekly hours per 12 months, unpaid. 2 weeks written notice required

### Unpaid leave
- No general statutory entitlement. PostHog's handbook allows maternity leave to be extended with unpaid time off by arrangement with your team and Fraser

## Relocations

### Moving TO the Netherlands
1. **Gemeente registration** — within 5 days of arrival. Book appointment immediately — wait times of 2-3 months in major cities
2. **BSN** — issued at registration. Unlocks bank account, health insurance, payroll
3. **Health insurance** — mandatory within 4 months. Compare at independer.nl or zorgwijzer.nl. PostHog provides private health insurance via Deel
4. **30% ruling** — submit within 4 months of start date (see below)
5. **DigiD** — set up for government services access

### Moving FROM the Netherlands
- **Deregister from BRP** (Personal Records Database) at gemeente — required if abroad for 8+ of next 12 months. Can be done 5 days before departure
- **Cancel health insurance** — at least 1 month before departure
- Failure to deregister can cause problems registering in another country

## Notice period

Varies by tenure. Notice is given per end of month.

| Tenure | Notice period |
|--------|--------------|
| <5 years | 1 month |
| 5-10 years | 2 months |
| 10-15 years | 3 months |
| 15+ years | 4 months |

- Employee's statutory notice is always **1 month** regardless of tenure (unless contract specifies longer)
- PostHog's default is **30 days** voluntary notice, which aligns with the Dutch statutory employee notice of 1 month

## Time tracking

- Not currently a statutory obligation in the Netherlands (unlike Germany and Spain)
- However, employers must be able to demonstrate compliance with working time rules: max **12 hours/day**, **60 hours/week**, averaging to **48 hours/week** over 16 weeks
- PostHog operates on trust and doesn't count hours or days worked, which is compliant in NL where time recording isn't mandatory

## 30% ruling

**This is the single most time-sensitive item in NL onboarding.** The 30% ruling is a tax advantage for employees recruited from abroad — up to 30% of gross salary paid tax-free for 5 years.

### Eligibility (2025)
- Recruited from abroad to work in the Netherlands
- Lived **>150 km** from Dutch border for at least **16 of 24 months** prior to start
- Minimum gross salary (after 30% reduction): **€46,660** (2025), or **€35,468** for under-30s with a master's degree
- Application must be submitted within **4 months** of employment start date for retroactive effect

### What's changing
- **2025-2026:** 30% rate applies (stable)
- **From 2027:** Rate drops to **27%** and salary thresholds increase ~9-10%
- Partial non-residency status **abolished as of Jan 2025** (transition for pre-2024 holders until 2026)
- Salary cap: 30% applies to max **€246,000** salary (2025)

### Application process
1. People Ops confirms eligibility at hire
2. Collect: valid ID, employment contract, work permit (if applicable), CV showing education/work history, signed application form (wet ink)
3. Submit to Belastingdienst within 4 months of start date
4. **If application is late:** benefit only starts from first of month after submission — retroactive application is lost

## Leaving PostHog

- **Voluntary:** 30 days notice (aligns with Dutch statutory 1 month for employees). Send resignation to people@posthog.com
- **Involuntary:** PostHog offers 4 months of base salary (includes any legally required notice period) for team members with 3+ months tenure
- Transition payment (transitievergoeding) is **mandatory under Dutch law** when employer terminates — even at contract end. Calculated as 1/3 monthly salary per year of service. This is a statutory minimum that applies regardless of PostHog's global severance policy
- Final payroll includes unused leave payout (calculated against 25-day assumption)
- Offboarding issue created from GitHub template by People & Ops
