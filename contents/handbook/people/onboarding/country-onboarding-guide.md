# Country onboarding guide

> **Owner:** People & Ops
> **Status:** Draft — external contribution
> **Last updated:** 2026-02-28

This guide covers the country-specific admin that changes depending on where a new team member lives. For the general onboarding process (buddy system, GitHub issue, in-person meetup), see the [main onboarding page](/handbook/people/onboarding).

## How to use this guide

1. Identify the team member's **employment track** (below)
2. Go to their **country page** for payroll blockers, right-to-work, leave, and compliance specifics
3. Complete the **universal onboarding** from the [main onboarding page](/handbook/people/onboarding) — this guide only covers the country-specific bits

## Employment tracks

| Track | Countries | Entity / Provider | Payroll cycle |
|-------|-----------|------------------|---------------|
| **Direct (US)** | United States | PostHog Inc | Twice monthly (15th + last day), via Deel |
| **Direct (UK)** | United Kingdom | PostHog Ltd | Monthly (last working day) |
| **Direct (DE)** | Germany | PostHog GmbH | Monthly |
| **EOR** | All other supported countries | Deel (Employer of Record) | Monthly (last working day) |
| **Contractor** | Where EOR isn't available | Deel (invoicing) | Monthly (upon invoice) |

**Excluded countries (high employer costs):** France, Italy, Sweden, Switzerland, Iceland, Belgium, Luxembourg, Uruguay, Bolivia, Denmark, Brazil.

**Sanctioned countries (cannot hire):** Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria.

**Timezone restriction:** GMT+2 through GMT-8 only.

## What's the same everywhere

Regardless of country or track, every new joiner needs:

- [ ] Company email set up
- [ ] Added to Deel with correct employment track
- [ ] Onboarding issue created in GitHub (from [company-internal template](https://github.com/PostHog/company-internal/blob/master/.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/))
- [ ] Onboarding buddy assigned (usually team lead or team member)
- [ ] Brex card issued
- [ ] In-person onboarding dates confirmed and travel coordinated
- [ ] Added to relevant Slack channels (see [onboarding page](/handbook/people/onboarding) for channel list)
- [ ] PTO by Deel app set up in Slack (connected to Google Calendar)
- [ ] Subscribed to team time off calendar

## Time off (all countries)

PostHog offers **unlimited, permissionless time off** with an expectation of **at least 25 days per year** (including national holidays). Time off is booked via the **PTO by Deel** Slack app — not on deel.com directly, as it doesn't sync with Slack. Team members manually book in public holidays they plan to take off using PTO by Deel's "Bulk Add by Region" feature.

## Parental leave (all countries)

PostHog's parental leave policy applies globally and is designed to be **more generous than local law** (not in addition to it). If local law provides more, the team member gets the local entitlement.

| Time at PostHog | Maternity leave | Paternity leave |
|----------------|----------------|----------------|
| Under 6 months | 3 weeks | 2-3 weeks |
| 6-12 months | 12 weeks | 2-3 weeks |
| Over 12 months | Up to 24 weeks | 6 weeks |

Communicate parental leave to Fraser at least **4 months** before it begins. Maternity leave can be extended using unpaid time off. Paternity leave cannot be extended with unpaid leave.

## Maintaining this guide

- **New country hire:** Add a page using the [country template](/handbook/people/onboarding/country-template)
- **Law changes:** Update the relevant country page. Deel flags most changes, but review quarterly
- **Deel provider changes:** Update benefits specifics, especially health insurance
- **Who owns this:** People & Ops. Review quarterly alongside goal-setting
