Customer Success Managers (CSMs) help customers get more value out of PostHog so they stick around. That means helping them onboard, train their team, work through support issues, find cost savings, and advocating for them inside PostHog.

## Principles

- **Show, don't tell.** Help customers prioritize their needs, deliver bad news early, and stay on top of their questions. Under promise, over deliver.
- **Help them save money, even when it costs us short term.** Long-term value matters more.
- **Be their voice inside PostHog.** Advocacy only works if you're willing to go to bat for them — push product and engineering to understand why a feature matters.

## Your first weeks

You'll spend most of your time managing your book of business and watching for churn signals. There should be **zero surprises** when a customer churns.

Start by understanding what each customer does, how they use PostHog today, and where you can add value. In priority order:

1. **Introduce yourself clearly.** Not everyone knows what a CSM does — especially long-time customers who haven't had a point of contact before.
2. **Triage your list for churn risk.** Subscribe to open Zendesk tickets for your customers so you get notified on updates.
3. **Set up Vitally alerts.** Use this <PrivateLink url="https://posthog.vitally-eu.io/settings/playbooks/f811379c-9b0b-4ff4-98d4-654df4f02d40">Playbook Template</PrivateLink> for new-conversation alerts. Also clone this <PrivateLink url="https://posthog.vitally-eu.io/hubs/152ccd4c-c7b2-4508-865b-b08fea5c3dc6/cc9ce419-fa0c-4d78-80e8-25e0760e459d">Open Tickets Tracker</PrivateLink>, filter by your CSM assignment, and save it.
4. **Get into customer Slack channels.** Add yourself to existing ones, and invite customers who aren't on Slack yet (with Pylon and your teammates). Email is one of the worst ways to reach our ICP. Telling customers you'll send a Slack Connect invite works much better than asking if they'd like to join. See [Shared Slack Channels with Customers](/handbook/growth/sales/slack-channels) for setup tips.

## Tips for initial engagement

Read [Getting people to talk to you](/handbook/growth/sales/getting-people-to-talk-to-you).

- **Be direct.** Tell customers you want to learn about their business and find ways to add value. Most are receptive.
- **Look at product usage before the call.** Which products are they on? Are there save opportunities? Cost savings are an easy opener.
- **Don't fixate on the champion.** Reach out to multiple team members during initial outreach — you don't know who'll be most responsive.
- **Bring an agenda to discovery calls.** Restate it on the call, end with action items, and gauge whether they'd want a recurring check-in.
