Support is enabled in your PostHog project settings. Once enabled, the widget and JavaScript API become available.

### How to enable

1. Go to **Settings > Conversations** in PostHog
2. Toggle **Enable conversations** on
3. Toggle **Enable widget** on to show the chat widget

The widget will automatically load on your site when you have `posthog-js` installed. You can also explicitly control this in your init config:

```javascript
posthog.init('YOUR_API_KEY', {
  disable_conversations: false // default - set to true to disable
})
```

<QuestLogItem

    subtitle="Recommended"
    icon="IconColor"
>

Make the widget match your brand and set up a greeting for your users.

In **Settings > Conversations**, configure:

- **Button color** - Match your brand (hex value)
- **Greeting message** - The welcome text users see when they open the widget
- **Allowed domains** - Restrict which domains can show the widget (optional)

You can also collect user information before they start chatting by enabling the identification form.

<QuestLogItem
    title="Test the widget"
    subtitle="Required"
    icon="IconFlask"
>

Visit your site and look for the chat button in the bottom-right corner. Click it to open the widget and send a test message.

### What to check

1. The widget appears on your site
2. You can open and close the chat panel
3. You can send a message
4. The greeting text shows correctly

If you don't see the widget, check that:
- Support is enabled in project settings
- The widget is enabled
- Your domain is in the allowed list (if you've set domain restrictions)
- `posthog-js` version is >= v1.230.0

</QuestLogItem>

<QuestLogItem
    title="View tickets in the inbox"
    subtitle="Required"
    icon="IconInbox"
>

After sending a test message, go to **Support** in PostHog to see your new ticket.

The inbox shows all tickets with:

- **Status** - New, open, pending, on hold, or resolved
- **Last message** - Preview of the most recent message
- **Unread count** - Messages you haven't seen yet

Click a ticket to view the full conversation and respond. Your response will appear in the user's widget.
