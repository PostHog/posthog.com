Exceptions can be suppressed by [configuring suppression rules](https://app.posthog.com/error_tracking?activeTab=configuration#selectedSetting=error-tracking-suppression-rules) in PostHog settings. If your rules only use client-safe properties, they are sent to SDKs and evaluated **client-side** for better performance. Rules that use properties requiring symbol resolution (like `$exception_sources` or `$exception_functions`) are evaluated **server-side** during ingestion, meaning matching exceptions are dropped before issue linking.

You can also configure a **sampling rate** (between 0.0 and 1.0) to suppress only a percentage of matching exceptions rather than all of them. For example, a sampling rate of 0.1 means 10% of matching exceptions will be captured.
