title: Summarize Surveys with PostHog AI
---

[PostHog AI](/docs/posthog-ai) analyzes and summarizes your [Survey](/docs/surveys) responses, extracting themes, sentiment, and actionable insights from open-text and structured answers. Instead of reading hundreds of responses manually, ask PostHog AI to surface the patterns.

## How it works

PostHog AI reads through your Survey responses and produces:

- **Theme extraction** – groups responses into common topics (e.g. "pricing concerns," "onboarding confusion," "missing features")
- **Sentiment analysis** – classifies feedback as positive, neutral, or negative
- **Actionable insights** – highlights specific suggestions and recurring requests
- **Segment comparisons** – differences in feedback between user groups (e.g. free vs paid, new vs returning)

You can ask follow-up questions to drill into specific themes, like "what do detractors say about pricing?" or "compare feedback from mobile vs desktop users."

## Example

When you ask PostHog AI to summarize an NPS Survey, it goes beyond the score. It analyzes open-text responses across the promoter, passive, and detractor groups to explain *why* users scored the way they did.

A typical NPS summary might include:

- **Promoters (9-10)** love the speed and ease of use, frequently mention the onboarding experience.
- **Passives (7-8)** are satisfied but want better documentation and more integrations.
- **Detractors (0-6)** cite pricing concerns and missing features like custom dashboards.

This turns a single number into actionable feedback you can act on.

## Tips for better results

- **Name the Survey** – "from the checkout feedback Survey" helps PostHog AI find the right dataset
- **Filter by segment** – "from enterprise users" or "from detractors only" narrows the analysis to the most relevant responses
- **Ask about specific themes** – "What do users say about pricing?" focuses the summary on a single topic
- **Request actionable output** – "What are the top three things we should fix?" prompts PostHog AI to prioritize its findings
- **Compare segments** – "How does feedback differ between free and paid users?" reveals differences you might not spot manually
