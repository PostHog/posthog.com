---
title: Using PostHog AI with Web Analytics
---

[PostHog AI](/docs/posthog-ai) can help you analyze web traffic, investigate anomalies, and understand visitor behavior – all through natural language prompts. Instead of building insights manually, describe what you want to know and PostHog AI queries your data for you.

## What you can do

- **Analyze traffic sources and patterns** – Break down visitors by referrer, UTM parameters, country, device, and more.
- **Investigate traffic spikes or drops** – Identify root causes behind sudden changes in traffic volume.
- **Understand visitor behavior by page** – Find pages with high bounce rates, low engagement, or declining traffic.
- **Query web analytics data with SQL** – PostHog AI writes and runs HogQL queries against your web analytics data.
- **Compare traffic across time periods** – Spot trends by comparing weeks, months, or custom date ranges.

## Example prompts

Try these prompts in [PostHog AI](/docs/posthog-ai):

- "Show me traffic sources for the last week"
- "What pages have the highest bounce rate?"
- "Compare this week's traffic to last week"
- "Which UTM campaigns drove the most conversions?"
- "Find pages with declining traffic this month"
- "Create a dashboard breaking down traffic by referral, country, and device"
- "Is there any suspicious traffic behavior in the last 30 days?"

For a full walkthrough showing how to investigate a traffic spike end-to-end – including identifying suspicious IPs and reviewing session replays – see [Investigate web traffic with PostHog AI](/docs/posthog-ai/investigate-web-traffic).

## Use with MCP

You can also query web analytics data through AI coding agents using the [PostHog MCP server](/docs/model-context-protocol). This lets you run analytics queries from tools like Cursor, Claude Code, and Windsurf without leaving your editor.
