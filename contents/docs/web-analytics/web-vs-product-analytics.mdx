Web analytics relies on a subset of the events and properties used by product analytics. This means you can use both in tandem.

## When should you use web analytics?

- You should use web analytics when you want a high-level overview of your site…
- Visitors: how many people are visiting?
- Paths: which pages are they visiting?
- Sources: what are the different sources of traffic to your website?
- Locations: where are visitors located?
- Device: did they use a desktop or tablet or phone?
- Browser: what browser were they using?
- Bounce rates
- Session durations
- Conversion goals: how many people are completing important tasks AKA converting
- Using web analytics helps you figure out what content to work on next, what products are most popular, etc…

## When should you use product analytics?

You should use product analytics when you want to dive deeper into the usage of your product.

- Analyze specific users or groups of users
- User cohorts
- retentions
- Building custom dashboards

Using product analytics helps you understand the usage of specific products, the behavior of specific users, etc…
