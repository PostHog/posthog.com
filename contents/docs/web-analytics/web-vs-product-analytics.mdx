- [Web analytics](/web-analytics) is for tracking website metrics. It's for marketing websites, blogs, or high volume B2C apps where users don't log in.
- [Product analytics](/product-analytics) for tracking your product. It's more powerful than web analytics. It's for web and mobile apps.

## When should you use web analytics?

Web analytics gives you a single, pre-built [dashboard](https://us.posthog.com/web) for a high-level overview of your site. It's about collecting data in the aggregate, as opposed to tracking specific interactions from specific users.

It can help you figure out which pages and products are most popular and resonate the most with people.

## When should you use product analytics?

When you want to dive deeper into the usage of your product, you should use product analytics.

![Product analytics examples](https://res.cloudinary.com/dmukukwp6/image/upload/f_webp,h_1080,w_1920,c_fill,g_auto,q_auto/v1/posthog.com/contents/templates/thumbnails/featured/prodcut-analytics?_a=AXAH4nI0)
<Caption>Product analtyics examples</Caption>

Product analytics is all about _how_ people are interacting with your product, as well as _who_ is using it. It gives you insights into metrics such as feature adoption rate, user retention, and churn.
